Vision vault

Whether you're a department head, office manager, or executive, the vision vault is your centralized hub of office management and operations.

By leveraging its insights, you can drive improvements, optimize workflows, and create a more productive and harmonious work environment, ultimately aiming to enhance efficiency, productivity, and the overall organization of workplace activities.

Obstacles and hurdles

The entire system and process was scattered. Documents, files, communication and information are dispersed across different department, platforms and devices. Process is varying team to team and lacking uniformity. More time and effort are being invested in administrative task. Data analytics like resource usage, team performance needs to prepare manually and shared to stakeholder. Overall, reporting was missing.

All-inclusive, this application can lead to better organizational efficiency, improved collaboration, and informed decision-making. However, its successful implementation requires careful planning, user-friendly design, and integration with existing systems to ensure maximum impact.

Design execution

Started with structured process to ensure that the final outcome should have potential to serve the business goal as well as each user goals efficiently. It is crucial to keep the user at the centre of the design and development efforts to ensure that the system provides a seamless experience for its users.

Journey started with understanding the purpose of the office management system. What are the specific objectives and goals needs to achieve with this system? Identify the primary user groups and their needs. Stepped in various design process like User research, Persona definition, workflows and journeys, wireframing and initial testing, Final visual mocks, testing and developer handoffs and finally vision vault shaped.

Design considerations for an office management system
  • Market analysis and various type of research -The system should be designed to be used by people with different levels of experience and abilities.
  • Design for different types of tasks -The system should be able to support a variety of office tasks, such as scheduling appointments, managing documents, and tracking projects and people activities.
  • Provide clear and concise feedback -The system should provide users with feedback on their actions to help them avoid errors.
  • Use a consistent design language -The system should use a consistent design language throughout to make it easier for users to learn and use.
  • Design for flexibility -The system should be designed to be flexible enough to adapt to changes in the office environment.

After effect

The adoption of vision vault an office management system brings forth a myriad of advantages, significantly enhancing organizational efficiency and productivity.
